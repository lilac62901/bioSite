about me

I was born in Odessa Ukraine and immigrated to the United States when I was four along with both of my parents. At first, we stayed with my Grandparents until we were able to get our own place in the city.

My childhood was actually pretty good. Pretty normal, if there is such a thing. I went to a lot of museums, amusement parks, sporting events, summer camps. Sundown was our beta version of an alarm clock for curfew. While I assimilated quickly to the cultural and lingual differences at school, at home we still spoke Russian and my parents had mostly Russian/Ukrainian friends. I think this helped create my curiosity around other cultures and the desire to learn about them.

I went to Southern Illinois University in Carbondale, Illinois. After school I moved to Chicago with my now wife where I worked in the Travel Industry, and she worked in Graphic Design. We were only there for a few years before we decided to quit our jobs, sell everything we owned and move to Japan. After Japan we moved back to Chicago for over a decade. While living there I experienced my childhood love, the Cubs, win the World Series.

My hobbies include following the news and current events, studying culture, and the Cubs. Also, I love studying Geography. I consider myself a budding futurist. And the Cubs. Music is also a passion of mine. Did I mention the Cubs?

My goal is to do something to benefit society in whatever capacity that could be achieved. Another goal is to invent a new form of art never before seen or heard of. Finally, I would love to live a happy life with my happy wife.
